# Pendahuluan
- the phylum Actinobacteria, class Actinobacteria, order Bifidobacteriales, in the family Bifidobacteriaceae.
- **Microbiologic Characteristics**:
  - Difficult to characterize.
  - Genus distinct by 16S rRNA sequence, related to Bifidobacterium (anaerobic gram-positive rods).
  - Characteristic
    - Facultatively anaerobic
    - oxidase- and catalase-negative
    - nonsporing
    - nonencapsulated
    - nonmotile
    - pleomorphic
    - gram-variable rod.
  - Thin cell wall, gram-variable/gram-negative appearance but likely gram-positive heritage.
# Tropisme
- **Habitat and Prevalence**:
  - Natural habitat: human vagina.
  - Universally present in women without vaginal infection signs or symptoms.
# Patho
- **Bacterial Vaginosis**:
  - Vaginal microbiota changes: decreased lactobacilli, predominance of G. vaginalis and other anaerobes.
  - G. vaginalis strains produce cytolysin and biofilm, role in pathogenesis unclear.
  - Novel unculturable bacteria species specific to bacterial vaginosis discovered.

- **Extravaginal Infections**:
  - Uncommon, mainly in women.
  - Associated with postpartum endometritis, fever, chorioamnionitis, septic abortion, cesarean section infection.
  - Rare urinary tract isolate, clinically significant in suprapubic bladder aspirates from pregnant women.
  - Rare osteoarticular infections in women, with or without prostheses.
  - Recovered from male urogenital tract, but sexual transmission evidence lacking.
  - Associated diseases in men: bacteremia, balanitis, urethritis, urinary infections, rare urosepsis and septicemia cases.

# Diagnosis
- **Diagnosis of Bacterial Vaginosis Associated with Gardnerella vaginalis or Mobiluncus**:
  - **Inspection Criteria**:
    1. Grayish and sometimes frothy vaginal discharge.
    2. Discharge with pH above 4.6.
    3. Amine (“fishy”) odor when alkalinized with potassium hydroxide.
    4. Presence of “clue cells” (large epithelial cells covered with gram-negative or gram-variable rods).
- **Growth Requirements**:
  - Does not grow on commercially available blood culture broths due to inhibition by sodium polyanethol sulfonate (SPS).
  - Requires SPS-free medium or medium with SPS supplemented with gelatin for suspected sepsis.
- **Isolation from Female Genital Tract Specimens**:
  - Best accomplished using human blood bilayer Tween (HBT) agar.
  - HBT agar:
    - Contains Columbia colistin-nalidixic acid (CNA) agar with amphotericin B to prevent yeast and fungal growth.
    - Human blood layered on top enhances the beta-hemolytic pattern of Gardnerella vaginalis.
    - Small colonies with diffuse zone of beta-hemolysis

# Manajemen
- **Treatment**:
  - Recommended: oral [[Metronidazole]], intravaginal [[Metronidazole]] gel, intravaginal [[Clindamycin]] cream.
  - Mixed anaerobic microbiota importance.
  - Treatment of sexual partners not recommended.
  - β-Lactams used for extravaginal infections.
