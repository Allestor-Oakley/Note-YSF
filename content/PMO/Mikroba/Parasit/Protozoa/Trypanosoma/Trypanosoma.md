# Pendahuluan
- The protozoan genus **Trypanosoma** has several dozen species.
- Two of the three species that infect humans are pathogenic.
- Several species cause severe, economically important diseases in domestic mammals.
- These organisms are protozoan flagellates from the family **Trypanosomatidae**, order **Kinetoplastida**.
- They pass through different morphologic stages: **epimastigote**, **amastigote**, and **trypomastigote** in vertebrate and invertebrate hosts.
- Not all species fulfill the criterion of three morphologic stages.
  - **Trypanosoma cruzi** (causes Chagas disease) and one other species multiply in mammalian hosts as intracellular amastigotes.
  - African trypanosomes (cause sleeping sickness) multiply as trypomastigotes in mammalian bloodstream and other extracellular spaces.
- **Trypomastigote** form:
  - Has a single flagellum originating near the kinetoplast.
  - Kinetoplast is a DNA-containing structure in the parasite's single, complex mitochondrion.
  - Flagellum runs alongside the body and extends beyond it as a free, threadlike structure.
  - Undulating membrane and the free flagellum portion provide motility.
### Development groups based on the vector
  1. **Stercoraria**: 
    - Multiplication in mammalian host is discontinuous (amastigote stage).
    - Development in vector (kissing bugs) completed in the hindgut (posterior station).
    - Transmission to mammalian hosts is contaminative.
    - Subgenus **Schizotrypanum** (includes [[Trypanosoma cruzi]]).
  2. **Salivaria**:
    - Multiplication in mammalian host is continuous (trypomastigote stage).
    - Development in vector (tsetse fly) completed in salivary glands (anterior station).
    - Transmission to mammalian hosts is inoculative.
    - Subgenus **Trypanozoon** (includes [[Trypanosoma brucei]] subspecies).
- **Trypanozoon** subgenus:
  - Includes subspecies **T. brucei brucei** (causes disease in animals, not humans).
  - **T. brucei gambiense** and **T. brucei rhodesiense** cause African sleeping sickness.
  - These three subspecies form the **T. brucei complex**.
- **Chagas disease** and **African sleeping sickness**:
  - Endemic areas do not overlap.
  - Differences in transmission, pathogenesis, and clinical course.
  - Share genetic and morphologic similarities of the causative agents.
