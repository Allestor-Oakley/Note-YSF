# Pendahuluan
- Leishmaniasis is a spectrum of clinical syndromes caused by protozoan parasites of the genus *Leishmania*, transmitted by the bite of a phlebotomine sand fly.
- Geographically, leishmaniasis is divided into Old World (Eastern Hemisphere) and New World (Western Hemisphere) diseases.
- The clinical manifestations vary widely but are often divided into three distinct syndromes: visceral leishmaniasis (VL), cutaneous leishmaniasis (CL), and mucosal leishmaniasis (ML).
- A single *Leishmania* species can produce more than one clinical syndrome, and each syndrome is caused by more than one species.
- The outcome in any patient results from the interplay of parasite factors (e.g., invasiveness, tropism, pathogenicity) and host factors (e.g., genetic predisposition, immune status, medication exposure).
- Infection can potentially lead to leishmaniasis, a heterogeneous collection of clinical diseases, each with its own unique geographic distribution, biology, ecology, local mammalian reservoir, and sand fly insect vector.
# Morfologi
- Leishmania organisms are dimorphic protozoa with two distinct morphologies within sand fly vectors and mammalian hosts.
- No clear sexual form has been identified, but sexual recombination is believed to occur.
- Although generally diploid, Leishmania organisms use aneuploidy as a mechanism of gene regulation.
- Promastigotes (1.5–3.5 µm × 15–200 µm) are elongate, flagellated, and motile forms found in the sand fly's digestive tract and proboscis.
- Intracellular promastigotes transform into oval or round amastigotes (1.5–3 µm × 3–5 µm) within 24 to 48 hours after infection.
- Amastigotes lack a visible flagellum on light microscopy and have a distinct, rod-shaped structure called a kinetoplast (a specialized mitochondrial structure).
- Visualization of a kinetoplast confirms the diagnosis of leishmaniasis.
# Transmisi
- Mammalian infection begins when a female sand fly regurgitates promastigotes into the bite site while taking a blood meal.
- Promastigotes are phagocytized by neutrophils, macrophages, and dendritic cells to avoid the innate immune response.
- Amastigotes multiply by binary division, rupture the cell, and invade other mononuclear phagocytes.
- Depending on the species and host factors, amastigotes may spread regionally or systemically from the bite site through lymphatics and/or the vascular system to infect mononuclear phagocytes elsewhere.
- The cycle is completed when female sand flies ingest parasitized cells.
- In the digestive tract of the sand flies, Leishmania parasites develop through a series of flagellated intermediate stages to become infectious metacyclic promastigotes over approximately 1 week.
- Transmission depends on the sand fly vector, the presence of a suitable reservoir, and susceptible humans.
- Female sand flies of the genera *Lutzomyia* and *Phlebotomus* transmit *Leishmania* species in the Americas and elsewhere, respectively.
- Phlebotomine sand flies are small, delicate insects ranging from 1.5 to 3.5 mm in length.
- Sand flies breed in the cracks of dwelling walls, rubbish, rubble, or rodent burrows.
- They are weak fliers and tend to remain close to the ground near their breeding sites and feed on nearby hosts.
- Sand flies make small cuts in host skin and feed on pooled blood.
- Saliva from the sand fly enhances the infectivity of promastigotes through its antihemostatic and immunomodulatory properties.
- Factors in the saliva, such as adenosine or maxadilan, influence the host immune response, favoring a Th2-type response along with decreased macrophage activation and diminished nitric oxide (NO) production.
- Transmission back to the sand fly occurs when a susceptible sand fly feeds on an infected reservoir host.
- It is unclear whether host parasitemia or parasites within the skin are responsible for this transmission.
- Sand fly saliva has been shown to increase host parasitemia after repeated feedings.
- The variability of sand fly infection seen in xenodiagnostic studies may be better accounted for by patchy skin infection.
- Depending on the *Leishmania* species, the sand fly genus, and the geographic location, the major reservoirs for leishmaniasis may be canines, rodents, or humans.
# Penyakit
Leishmaniasis is an infection by the protozoan parasites in the genus Leishmania and causes three primary clinical syndromes: 
- visceral leishmaniasis (VL)
- cutaneous leishmaniasis (CL) 
- mucosal leishmaniasis (ML)
# Patho
- Leishmaniasis, similar to leprosy, can be thought of as a polar disorder.
- **Polyparasitic Infections**: Includes diffuse cutaneous leishmaniasis (CL) or visceral leishmaniasis (VL) characterized by a Th2-type immune response, similar to lepromatous leprosy.
  - **Diagnosis**: Heavily parasitized macrophages are abundant; diagnosis is readily made by smear.
- **Oligoparasitic Infections**: Includes mucocutaneous leishmaniasis (ML) and latent VL infection, characterized by a Th1-type immune response, similar to tuberculoid leprosy.
  - **Diagnosis**: Amastigotes are sparse; mononuclear cell infiltrate predominates.
- **Immune Response Complexity**: Interaction between Th1 and Th2 includes variable modulation by Th17-type CD4+ T cells.
- **Visceralization Factors**: The A2 gene family is important in visceralization and protection from heat shock and oxidative stress.
  - **L. donovani**: Produces more A2, contributing to its ability to cause VL.
  - **L. major**: Insertion of L. donovani-specific genes promotes survival in viscera of mice in murine models.
- **Intracellular Survival**: Essential for all forms of leishmaniasis pathogenesis.
  - **Virulence Factors**: Include lipo-phosphoglycan and leishmanolysin (GP63).
    - **Lipo-Phosphoglycan**: Aids in transformation of phagosomes into parasitophorous vacuoles.
    - **Leishmanolysin (GP63)**: Facilitates complement and natural killer cell inactivation, prevents antigen cross-presentation to T cells, and modulates macrophage signaling and transcription pathways.

# Diagnosis
- **Diagnosis Approaches for Leishmania Infections**:
  1. **Clinical**: Combines epidemiology and clinical manifestations but is rarely adequate on its own.
  2. **Parasitologic**:
     - Visualizing amastigotes in tissue biopsy or smear.
     - Visualizing promastigotes in culture.
     - Amplifying Leishmania-specific nucleic acids by PCR.
     - Parasites should be speciated using isoenzyme techniques or molecular methods (e.g., multilocus sequence typing, PCR).
     - Specimens for culture maintained at 22°C to 26°C.
     - Parasite culture and assays mainly performed in reference labs.
     - CDC provides culture media and expert assistance in the U.S.
  3. **Immunologic**:
     - Various antibody tests, cytokine release assays, and the leishmanin skin test.
     - Tests evaluate cell-mediated immune responses.
     - Not standardized or commercially available.
- **Optimal Diagnostic Test**: Depends on the parasite burden of the leishmaniasis syndrome.
- **Guidelines**: The IDSA and ASTMH recommend a multipronged approach, using molecular methods whenever possible.
