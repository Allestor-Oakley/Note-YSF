# Morfologi
[[Aspergillus#Morfologi]]
- **Colony Appearance**: Colonies are typically gray-green with a wooly to cottony texture.
- **Hyphae Characteristics**: Hyphae are hyaline (lightly pigmented), have septa, and usually branch at acute (typically 45-degree) angles.
- **Conidial Head**: The conidial head is columnar with conidiophores that are smooth-walled and uncolored, or darkened near the vesicle.
- **Uniseriate Structure**: This species is uniseriate, with closely compacted phialides borne only on the upper portion of the vesicle.
- **Conidia**: Conidia are smooth to finely roughened and are 2 to 3.5 µm in diameter.
- **Fruiting Head Visibility**: The fruiting head (conidiophore and conidia) is not commonly seen in clinical specimens but may be detected in sites exposed to air, such as wounds or lung cavities.
