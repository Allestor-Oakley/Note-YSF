# Morfologi
[[Aspergillus#Morfologi]]
- **Habitat**: Found in soil and decaying vegetation.
- **Colony Appearance**: Colonies are olive to lime green and grow rapidly.
- **Seriation**: Some isolates are uniseriate but more typically biseriate.
- **Metulae and Phialides**: In biseriate species, sterile cells known as metulae are attached to the vesicle and support the phialides.
- **Conidiophores and Conidia**: A. flavus has rough conidiophores and smooth conidia (3–6 µm).
- **Morphological Identification**: If characterized by morphology alone, A. flavus and other species in the section Flavi should be referred to as members of the Aspergillus flavus species complex.
- **Other Species**: Other species, such as A. alliaceus, have been reported to cause clinical infections and may have decreased susceptibility to polyenes.

# Penyakit
[[Aspergillus#Penyakit]]
- **Common Isolate**: Aspergillus flavus is commonly found in sinusitis, skin, and other invasive infections.
- **Aflatoxin Production**: This species produces [[Aflatoxin]].
