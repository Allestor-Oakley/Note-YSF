# Morfologi
[[Aspergillus#Morfologi]]
- **Habitat**: Aspergillus niger is found in soil, on plants, and in food and condiments like pepper.
- **Colony Appearance**: Colonies are initially white but quickly turn black with the production of pigmented fruiting structures; they grow rapidly with a pale yellow reverse.
- **Conidial Heads**: Conidial heads are biseriate and cover the entire vesicle.
- **Conidia**: Conidia are brown to black, very rough (4–5 µm), although the hyphae are hyaline.
- **Oxalate Crystals**: The species may produce oxalate crystals in clinical specimens.
- **Pathogenicity**: The role in invasive infection is less well established; decreased pathogenicity may be due to larger conidia not reaching deep lung tissues.
- **Common Isolate**: It is a common colonizing isolate and can cause superficial infections like otitis externa.
- **Species Complex**: Contains related species, including A. tubingensis, which may have a role in invasive infection.
