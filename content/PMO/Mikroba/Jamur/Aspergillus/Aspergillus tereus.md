# Morfologi
[[Aspergillus#Morfologi]]
- **Conidia Size**: Conidia are small (2.0–2.5 µm).
- **Colony Appearance**: Colonies range in color from buff to beige to cinnamon.
- **Conidiophores and Conidial Heads**: Conidiophores are smooth-walled and hyaline; conidial heads are biseriate and columnar.
- **Distinguishing Feature**: Presence of globose accessory conidia produced on hyphae, detectable in histopathologic samples.
- **Molecular Characterization**: A. terreus is a species complex; species such as A. alabamensis and A. carneus also cause infections in immunocompromised hosts and are less susceptible to polyene antifungals.

# Penyakit
[[Aspergillus#Penyakit]]
- **Common Isolate**: Aspergillus terreus is a common soil-related isolate.
- **Invasive Infection**: Increasingly reported in invasive infections in immunocompromised hosts.
- **Identification Importance**: Important due to resistance to antifungals, including amphotericin B, though improved susceptibility with newer azoles has been reported.
