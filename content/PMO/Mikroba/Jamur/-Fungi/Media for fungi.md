**Primary Recovery Media**

| **Media**                                    | **Purpose**                                          | **Ingredients**                                                                                                                                                                      | **Details**                                                                                                                                                      |
|----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Brain-heart infusion agar**                | Primary recovery of saprobic and pathogenic fungi    | Brain-heart infusion, enzymatic digest of animal tissue, enzymatic digest of casein, dextrose, sodium chloride                                                                       | Provides a rich medium for bacteria, yeast, and pathogenic fungi                                                                                                  |
| **Brain-heart infusion agar with antibiotics** | Primary recovery of pathogenic fungi exclusive of dermatophytes | Brain-heart infusion, enzymatic digest of animal tissue, enzymatic digest of casein, dextrose, sodium chloride, 10% sheep blood, antibiotics (chloramphenicol, cycloheximide, gentamicin) | Rich medium for yeast and pathogenic fungi, including systemic dimorphic fungi                                                                                   |
| **Chromogenic agar**                         | Isolation and presumptive identification of yeast and filamentous fungi | Chromopeptone, glucose, chromogen mix, chloramphenicol                                                                                                                             | Chromogen mix reacts with enzymes produced by different organisms, resulting in characteristic color changes                                                      |
| **Dermatophyte test medium**                 | Primary recovery of dermatophytes; recommended as screening medium  | Soy, peptone, dextrose, cycloheximide, gentamicin, chloramphenicol, phenol red                                                                                                    | Dermatophytes produce alkaline metabolites, raising the pH and changing the medium from red to yellow                                                             |
| **Inhibitory mold agar**                     | Primary recovery of pathogenic, cycloheximide-sensitive fungi exclusive of dermatophytes | Chloramphenicol, casein, dextrose, starch, sodium phosphate, magnesium sulfate, sodium chloride, manganese sulfate                                                | Chloramphenicol inhibits bacterial growth                                                                                                                        |
| **Potato flake agar**                        | Primary recovery of saprobic and pathogenic fungi and stimulation of conidia formation | Potato flakes, glucose, cycloheximide, chloramphenicol, bromthymol blue                                                                                          | Growth enhanced by pH alkaline reaction of fungus; chloramphenicol and antibiotics inhibit bacteria and nonpathogenic fungi                                        |
| **Mycobiotic or mycosel agar**               | Primary recovery of dermatophytes but may also be used for recovery of other pathogenic fungi | Pancreatic digest of soybean meal, dextrose with cycloheximide, chloramphenicol                                                                                         | Inhibits bacteria and saprophytic fungi                                                                                                                           |
| **Sabouraud dextrose with brain-heart infusion (SABHI) agar** | Primary recovery of saprobic and pathogenic fungi              | Sabouraud dextrose, brain-heart infusion agar, chloramphenicol, cycloheximide, penicillin, streptomycin, 10% sheep blood (optional)                           | Isolates and enhances growth of all fungi, including yeast phase of dimorphic fungi                                                                               |
| **Yeast-extract phosphate agar with ammonia**| Primary recovery of pathogenic fungi exclusive of dermatophytes  | Yeast extract, dipotassium phosphate, chloramphenicol, ammonium hydroxide (applied to the agar surface)                                                              | Enhances recovery and sporulation of *Blastomyces* and *Histoplasma capsulatum* from contaminated specimens                                                      |

**Differential Test Media**

| **Media**                      | **Purpose**                                                            | **Ingredients**                              | **Details**                                                              |
|--------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Acetate Ascospore agar**     | Detection of ascospores in ascosporegenous yeasts (e.g., *Saccharomyces* spp.) | Potassium acetate, yeast extract, dextrose   | Potassium acetate is necessary; yeast extract increases sporulation of yeasts |
| **Christensen’s urea agar**    | Identification of *Cryptococcus, Trichosporon,* and *Rhodotorula* spp. | 2% urea, phenol red                          | Produces urease and a change in the pH                                    |
| **Cornmeal agar with Tween 80 and trypan blue** | Differentiation of *Candida* spp. by chlamydospore production    | Cornmeal, Tween 80, cornmeal infusion, trypan blue | Addition of Tween 80 enhances chlamydospore, pseudohyphal and arthrospore formation; trypan blue provides a contrasting background |
| **Czapek’s agar**              | Differential identification of *Aspergillus* spp.                      | Sodium nitrate, sucrose, yeast extract       | Produces characteristic features of yeast and fungus of any organism that can use sodium nitrate |
| **Niger seed agar (birdseed agar)** | Identification of *Cryptococcus* spp., particularly *Cryptococcus neoformans* and *Cryptococcus gattii* | *Guizotia abyssinica* seeds or niger seeds, dextrose, creatinine, chloramphenicol | *C. neoformans* and *C. gattii* produce phenol oxidase, resulting in brown pigment from caffeic acid metabolism; creatinine enhances melanization |
| **Potato dextrose agar**       | Demonstration of conidia formation and pigment production by *Trichophyton rubrum* | Potato infusion, dextrose, tartaric acid | Carbohydrate and potato infusion promote growth of yeasts and molds; low pH inhibits bacterial growth |
| **Trichophyton agars 1–7**     | Identification of *Trichophyton* spp.                                  | Dextrose, monopotassium phosphate, magnesium sulfate, amino acids: 1. Casamino acids; vitamin free 2. Casamino acids plus inositol 3. Casamino acids plus inositol and thiamine 4. Casamino acids plus thiamine 5. Casamino acids plus niacin 6. Ammonium nitrate 7. Ammonium nitrate plus histidine | *Trichophyton* spp. differentiated by growth in presence of different amino acids |
| **Yeast fermentation broth**   | Identification of yeasts by determining fermentation                   | Yeast extract, peptone, bromcresol purple, specific carbohydrate (e.g., dextrose, maltose, sucrose) | Most yeasts produce acid, indicated by a change from purple to yellow as a positive fermenter |
| **Yeast nitrogen-base agar**   | Identification of yeasts by determining carbohydrate assimilation       | Ammonium sulfate, carbon source (e.g., glucose, sucrose, raffinose) | Assimilation of carbon by yeast cells produces a positive result |
