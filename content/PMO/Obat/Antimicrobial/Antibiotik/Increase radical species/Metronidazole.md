# Definisi singkat
Metronidazole is a commonly used antibiotic, belonging to the nitroimidazole class of antibiotics. It is frequently used to treat gastrointestinal infections as well as trichomoniasis and giardiasis, and amebiasis which are parasitic infections. Metronidazole has been used as an antibiotic for several decades, with added antiparasitic properties that set it apart from many other antibacterial drugs, allowing it to treat a wide variety of infections. It is available in capsule form, tablet form, and topical form, and suppository preparations for the treatment of various infections.

# MOA
- **Mechanism Not Fully Established**:
  - It’s possible that an intermediate in the reduction of metronidazole, only produced by anaerobic bacteria and protozoa, binds to DNA and electron-transport proteins, blocking nucleic acid synthesis.
- **Cell Entry**:
  - Metronidazole enters cells by passive diffusion.
- **Activation and Reduction**:
  - Once inside, ferredoxin or flavodoxin reduces the nitro group to nitro radicals.
  - In the cytoplasm of anaerobic bacteria, metronidazole is activated and reduced into a short-lived nitroso free radical.
- **Selectivity**:
  - The redox potential of electron transport portions in anaerobic or microaerophilic microorganisms makes metronidazole selective to these organisms, causing nitro group reduction and producing toxic metabolites (N-(2-hydroxyethyl) oxamic acid and acetamide).
- **DNA Interaction and Damage**:
  - The nitroso free radical interacts with DNA, causing a loss of the helical structure and strand breakage.
  - This results in bacterial death and potential damage to DNA of replicating organisms.
# Indikasi
- **Confirmed Trichomoniasis**
  - Caused by *Trichomonas vaginalis*.
  - Except for use in the first trimester of pregnancy.
  - Treats both the patient and their sexual partners.
- **Bacterial Vaginosis**
- **Certain Types of Amebiasis**
- **Various Anaerobic Infections**
  - May occur on:
    - Skin and skin structures
    - Abdomen
    - Heart
    - Reproductive organs
    - Central nervous system
    - Respiratory system
  - May be present in the bloodstream in cases of septicemia.
  - Common infections include:
    - *Bacteroides* species infections
    - *Clostridium* infections
    - *Fusobacterium* infections
    - *Peptococcus* infections
    - *Peptostreptococcus* infections
- **Topical Use**
  - Treatment of inflammatory lesions of rosacea.
- **Off-Label Uses**
  - Treatment of Crohn's disease.
  - Prophylactic agent after surgery.
  - Treatment of *Helicobacter pylori* infection.
  - Studied for:
    - Prevention of preterm births.
    - Treatment of periodontal disease.
# Farmakodinamik
- **Antimicrobial Activity**:
  - Treats amebiasis, trichomoniasis, and giardiasis.
  - Exerts both antibacterial and antiprotozoal activities.
  - Effective against some anaerobic bacterial infections.
  - Antibacterial activity against the majority of obligate anaerobes.
  - Does not demonstrate significant action against facultative anaerobes or obligate aerobes in vitro.
  - Nitro group reduction by anaerobic organisms likely responsible for antimicrobial cytotoxic effects, causing DNA strand damage to microbes.
- **Risks and Precautions**:
  - **Peripheral Neuropathy and Convulsions**:
    - Risk increases, especially at higher doses.
    - Discontinue use if convulsions or numbness of an extremity occur.
  - **Carcinogenesis**:
    - Found to be carcinogenic in mice and rats.
    - Relevance to humans is unknown.
    - Administer only when clinically necessary and for approved indications.
# Farmakokinetik
## Absorption
- **Intravenous Administration**:
  - **1.5g Dose**:
    - Peak concentration reached within 1 hour.
    - Peak level: 30-40 mg/L.
  - **500mg Dose, three times a day**:
    - Steady-state concentrations achieved within about 3 days.
    - Peak concentration: 26 mg/L.
- **Oral Administration**:
  - Completely absorbed.
  - Bioavailability: >90%.
  - **500mg Single Oral Dose**:
    - Cmax: 8 to 13 mg/L.
    - Tmax: 25 minutes to 4 hours.
    - AUC: 122 ± 10.3 mg/L•h.
- **Topical Administration**:
  - Insignificant percutaneous absorption.
  - **1% Metronidazole Cream**:
    - 100 mg dose of 14C-labelled metronidazole 2% cream applied to unbroken skin.
    - After 12 hours, no metronidazole detected in plasma.
    - Approximately 0.1% to 1% of administered dose measured in urine and feces.
## Distribution
- Metronidazole is less than 20% bound to plasma proteins.
- **Body Fluids**:
  - Widely distributed in:
    - Bile
    - Saliva
    - Breastmilk
    - Cerebrospinal fluid
    - Placenta
- **Volume of Distribution**:
  - Steady-state volume distribution in adults ranges from 0.51 to 1.1 L/kg.
- **Tissue Concentrations**:
  - Attains 60 to 100% of plasma concentrations in various tissues, including the central nervous system.
  - Not measured in high concentrations in the placental tissue.
## Metabolism
- **Metabolic Pathways**:
  - Undergoes hepatic metabolism via hydroxylation, oxidation, and glucuronidation.
  - Yields 5 metabolites.
- **Major Active Metabolite**:
  - Hydroxy metabolite: 1-(2-hydroxy-ethyl)-2-hydroxy methyl-5-nitroimidazole.
- **Unchanged Metronidazole**:
  - Found in the plasma along with small amounts of its 2-hydroxymethyl metabolite.
- **Metabolites in Urine**:
  - Several metabolites found, primarily products of side-chain oxidation and glucuronide conjugation.
  - Only 20% of the dose found in urine is accounted for by unchanged metronidazole.
- **Main Oxidative Metabolites**:
  - Hydroxy metabolites
  - Acetic acid metabolites
## EEC
- Metronidazole and metabolites are 60 to 80% eliminated in the urine, and 6-15% excreted in the feces.
- The elimination half-life of metronidazole is 7.3 ± 1.0 after a single 500mg IV dose in healthy subjects. Another resource indicates that the elimination half-life for metronidazole ranges from 6 to 10 hours
- Dose adjustments may be required in patients with hepatic impairment, as clearance is impaired in these patients. The clearance of metronidazole in the kidneys is estimated at 10 mL/min/1.73 m2. The total clearance from serum is about 2.1 to 6.4 L/h/kg
# Dosis
### Trichomoniasis (adults)
Treatment should be individualized. Usual dosages are:
- One-day treatment: 2 grams as a single oral dose or a split dose given on the same day
- 7-day course: 250mg three times daily for 7 consecutive days.
### Amebiasis
- Adults: acute amebic dysentery. 750mg three times daily for 5 to 10 days.
- Adults: amebic liver abscess. 500mg or 750mg three times daily for 5 to 10 days.
- Children: 35mg/kg/24 hours to 50mg/kg/24 hours divided into three doses daily for 10 days.
Do not exceed adult dosages.
### Anaerobic Bacterial Infections (adults)
For more serious anaerobic infections, intravenous metronidazole is usually administered initially.
- Adults: 7.5 mg/kg every 6 hours (this works out at approximately 500mg for a 70kg adult) orally for 7 to 10 days. Maximum dose 4g/24 hours.
A longer course of treatment may be required for bone, joint, lower respiratory tract, and endocardium infections.
# Interaksi
## Makanan
-  Avoid alcohol. Unpleasant symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal distress may occur with alcohol.
- Take with or without food. The extended release formulation should, however, be taken without food.
# Efek samping
#### Emergency Medical Help Needed for:
- **Allergic Reactions**:
  - Hives, itching, warmth, or tingling
  - Fever, joint pain
  - Dry mouth, dry vagina
  - Stuffy nose
  - Difficulty breathing
  - Swelling in face or throat
  - Severe skin reaction (fever, sore throat, burning eyes, skin pain, red or purple rash with blistering and peeling)
#### Serious Side Effects:
- **Nervous System Problems**:
  - Brain disorder (encephalopathy)
  - Inflammation of brain and spinal cord membranes (aseptic meningitis)
  - Numbness or tingling in hands or feet (peripheral neuropathy)
  - Seizures (convulsions)
- **Worsening Yeast Infection**:
  - Symptoms in people with a known or undiagnosed yeast infection (candidiasis)
- **Low White Blood Cell Count** (Leukopenia):
  - In people with a history of blood problems
  - Affects body's ability to fight infection
#### Common Side Effects:
- Diarrhea
- Nausea
- Headache
- Pain in the upper abdomen and abdominal cramping
- Vomiting
- Weight loss
- Constipation
#### Other Potential Side Effects:
- **Allergic Reactions**:
  - Itching, hives (urticaria)
  - Flushing of the skin
  - Red skin rash, blisters, and separation of skin layers
  - Nasal congestion
  - Dryness of mouth and vagina
  - Fever
- **Abnormal Heart Rhythms** (QT prolongation)
- **Liver Problems in People with Cockayne Syndrome**:
  - Life-threatening liver problems
  - Signs of liver failure: nausea, upper right side stomach pain, dark urine, clay-colored stools, jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)
# Kontraindikasi
- have ever had an allergic reaction to metronidazole or any other medicine
- are pregnant or breastfeeding
- have liver problems
- are having dialysis
- feel you will not be able to stop drinking alcohol while using metronidazole
