
## **Papillomaviridae – Human Papillomavirus (HPV)**

---

### **1. Introduction**

**Human Papillomaviruses (HPVs)** are a diverse group of **non-enveloped, double-stranded DNA viruses** that infect **epithelial tissues** of the skin and mucous membranes. There are over **200 HPV types**, categorized based on oncogenic potential into:

- **Low-risk types**: (e.g., HPV-6, 11) – cause benign lesions like **warts** and **genital condylomas**
    
- **High-risk types**: (e.g., HPV-16, 18, 31, 33, 45) – associated with **cervical**, **anal**, **oropharyngeal**, and other **anogenital cancers**
    

HPV is **transmitted through direct contact**, including sexual, perinatal, and possibly fomite-mediated routes.

---

### **2. Virological Profile / Characteristics**

- **Family**: Papillomaviridae
    
- **Genus**: Multiple genera (Alphapapillomavirus most clinically relevant)
    
- **Genome**:
    
    - Circular **double-stranded DNA (~8 kb)**
        
    - Encodes **early (E)** and **late (L)** proteins
        

#### **Structural Characteristics**:

- **Non-enveloped**, icosahedral capsid (~55 nm)
    
- Two capsid proteins:
    
    - **L1**: Major capsid protein (used in vaccines)
        
    - **L2**: Minor capsid protein (involved in DNA encapsidation)
        

#### **Genomic Regions**:

- **Early genes (E1–E7)**: Viral replication, transcriptional regulation, oncogenesis
    
- **Late genes (L1, L2)**: Structural components
    
- **Long Control Region (LCR)**: Regulatory elements (origin of replication, promoters)
    

#### **Key Oncoproteins**:

- **E6**: Binds and degrades **p53**, inhibiting apoptosis
    
- **E7**: Binds and inactivates **Rb protein**, promoting S-phase entry
    
- **E1/E2**: DNA replication and regulation of transcription
    

---

### **3. Pathophysiology**

#### **A. Entry and Initial Infection**

- Infection occurs at **microabrasions** in **basal epithelial cells** of skin or mucosa
    
- **L1 protein** binds to heparan sulfate proteoglycans on the cell surface
    
- Virus enters via **clathrin-mediated endocytosis**
    

#### **B. Replication and Life Cycle**

1. **Early phase** in basal cells:
    
    - Viral DNA remains **episomal**
        
    - Expression of **E1, E2** for replication
        
    - Low levels of **E6, E7** to maintain host cell proliferation
        
2. **Productive phase** in differentiating suprabasal layers:
    
    - Activation of **late promoters**
        
    - Expression of **L1, L2**
        
    - Assembly of viral particles
        
3. **Shedding** of mature virions occurs from surface epithelial cells
    

#### **C. Oncogenic Progression**

- In **high-risk HPV** types:
    
    - **Integration** of viral DNA into host genome disrupts **E2** gene (a repressor of E6/E7)
        
    - Overexpression of **E6** and **E7** → Unregulated cell cycle → Genomic instability → **Malignant transformation**
        

---

### **4. Clinical Manifestations**

#### **A. Cutaneous Warts**

- Common warts (HPV-2, 4)
    
- Plantar warts (HPV-1)
    
- Flat warts (HPV-3, 10)
    

#### **B. Anogenital Lesions**

- **Condyloma acuminata** (HPV-6, 11): Soft, cauliflower-like genital warts
    
- **Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)**: Graded CIN 1–3 based on dysplasia
    
- **Anal, vulvar, vaginal, penile, and oropharyngeal intraepithelial neoplasia**
    

#### **C. Malignancies**

- **Cervical cancer**: ~99% linked to HPV (esp. 16, 18)
    
- **Anal cancer**: Up to 90% HPV-associated
    
- **Oropharyngeal cancers**: ~70% related to HPV (especially in non-smokers)
    

---

### **5. Complications**

- **Malignant transformation** of precancerous lesions
    
- **Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP)**:
    
    - Caused by vertical transmission of HPV-6/11 to neonates
        
    - Leads to benign tumors in respiratory tract
        

---

### **6. Diagnosis and Differential Diagnosis**

#### **A. Clinical Evaluation**

- Warts: Typically diagnosed clinically
    
- Genital warts: May require biopsy if uncertain
    

#### **B. HPV DNA Testing (Molecular)**

- **PCR**: Detects and types HPV DNA
    
- **Hybrid Capture 2 (HC2)**: Semi-quantitative test detecting 13–14 high-risk HPV types
    

#### **C. Cytology**

- **Pap smear (cervical cytology)**:
    
    - Detects dysplastic changes (koilocytes)
        
    - Bethesda System classification (ASCUS, LSIL, HSIL)
        

#### **D. Histology**

- **Koilocytosis**: Enlarged, irregular nuclei with perinuclear halos
    
- Dysplasia and neoplasia graded in biopsies (CIN I–III)
    

#### **E. Colposcopy**

- Acetic acid application highlights abnormal areas (white epithelium)
    
- Used with directed biopsy
    

#### **F. Differential Diagnosis**

|Condition|Distinguishing Features|
|---|---|
|**Molluscum contagiosum**|Umbilicated lesions, poxvirus, central dimpling|
|**Condyloma lata (syphilis)**|Smooth, moist, flat, treponemal serology positive|
|**Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC)**|Ulcerated, indurated lesions, biopsy confirmation|
|**Herpes simplex virus (HSV)**|Painful vesicles, Tzanck smear, PCR for HSV|

---

### **7. Treatment and Management**

#### **A. Warts and Lesions**

- **Topical**:
    
    - Podophyllin, podofilox
        
    - Imiquimod (immune modulator)
        
    - Trichloroacetic acid (TCA)
        
- **Surgical**:
    
    - Cryotherapy
        
    - Electrosurgery
        
    - Laser ablation
        
    - Excision
        

#### **B. Dysplasia and Neoplasia**

- **CIN 1**: Often monitored (spontaneous regression)
    
- **CIN 2–3**: Requires excisional treatment:
    
    - LEEP (Loop Electrosurgical Excision Procedure)
        
    - Cold knife conization
        
    - Ablative therapies (cryotherapy, laser)
        

#### **C. Cancer Management**

- Based on staging; may involve surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, or immunotherapy (e.g., checkpoint inhibitors)
    

---

### **8. Prevention**

#### **A. Vaccination**

**Prophylactic vaccines** (virus-like particles from L1 protein):

- **Bivalent** (HPV-16, 18): Cervarix
    
- **Quadrivalent** (HPV-6, 11, 16, 18): Gardasil
    
- **Nonavalent** (Gardasil 9): Covers HPV-6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58
    

**Recommended ages**:

- Girls and boys: 9–14 years (2 doses)
    
- Older adolescents and adults up to age 26 (catch-up); some guidelines extend to 45
    

#### **B. Screening**

- **Pap smear**: Every 3 years starting at age 21
    
- **HPV co-testing**: Every 5 years after 30 (preferred)
    
- **Follow-up** of abnormal results per ASCCP guidelines
